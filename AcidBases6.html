Space for Titration Apparatus Diagram

(i) Calculate the volume of 0.0625 mol/dm³ dilute sulfuric acid, H₂SO₄, that completely reacts with 25.0 cm³ of 0.100 mol/dm³ potassium hydroxide, KOH, to produce aqueous potassium sulfate.

Tap to reveal answer

Moles of KOH = 0.100 x (25.0/1000) = 0.0025 mol.

The mole ratio is now 1:1, so twice the amount of acid is needed for the same amount of KOH. Volume = 20 x 2 = 40 cm³.

This question is about sulfur and compounds of sulfur.

(a) (i) Balance the equation for the reaction occurring when iron pyrites reacts with oxygen in the air.

....FeS₂ + .....O₂ → .....Fe₂O₃ + .....SO₂

Tap to reveal answer

4FeS₂ + 11O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃ + 8SO₂

(ii) Name Fe₂O₃. Include the oxidation number of iron.

Tap to reveal answer

Iron(III) oxide

(b) The forward reaction is exothermic. Using explanations that do not involve cost:

(i) explain why a temperature greater than 450°C is not used

Tap to reveal answer

A higher temperature would shift the equilibrium to the left (favouring the endothermic direction), which would decrease the yield of SO₃.

(ii) explain why a pressure lower than 2 atm is not used.

Tap to reveal answer

A lower pressure would shift the equilibrium to the left (the side with more moles of gas), which would decrease the yield of SO₃. Alternatively, a lower pressure would decrease the rate of reaction.

Tap to reveal answer

Tap to reveal answer

(iii) Describe how pure lead(II) sulfate can be obtained from the mixture.

Tap to reveal answer
