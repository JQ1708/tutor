  • There will be more extreme weather events.

Questions about trends in Group 1 metals (e.g., Sodium).

Check Your Progress 2.2

Draw a diagram to show the arrangement of electrons in a fluorine ion (F⁻).

Answer

The diagram should show a nucleus, and two electron shells with the structure 2,8 (gaining one electron to the original 2,7 structure).

Check Your Progress 2.3

a) Why is a fluorine ion more stable than a fluorine atom? b) Which element is most reactive in Group 1?

Answer

  • a) A fluorine ion is more stable because its outer (highest energy level) shell of electrons is full.
  • b) The most reactive element in Group 1 is Francium (Fr).

Check Your Progress 2.4

Identify the bonding in these substances: Sodium chloride, Methane (CH₄), Carbon dioxide, Magnesium oxide, Lithium fluoride.

Answer

  • a) Sodium chloride: ionic
  • b) Methane (CH₄): covalent
  • c) Carbon dioxide: covalent
  • d) Magnesium oxide: ionic
  • e) Lithium fluoride: ionic

Check Your Progress 2.5

a) What holds the ions together in sodium chloride? b) What does this mean for its melting and boiling points?

Answer

  • a) The strong electrostatic forces between the positive sodium ions (Na⁺) and the negative chloride ions (Cl⁻).
  • b) The melting and boiling points will be high because these electrostatic forces are strong and require a lot of energy to overcome.
