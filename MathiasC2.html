üß† What is Excretion?

Let's break it down. Think of your body like a busy factory.

Excretion is the process of getting rid of waste substances from an organism.

These aren't just any wastes! They are substances the body has made inside its cells. This is different from getting rid of undigested food (which is egestion).

It's so important that it's one of the seven life processes (Remember MRS GREN?).

üó∫Ô∏è Organs & Structure

The excretory system, also known as the renal system, is a team of organs. Here are the main players!

Meet the Team

  • Kidney: The main filter! Its job is to filter the blood and remove wastes like urea.
  • Ureter: The transport tube. There are two of them, one from each kidney. They carry urine from the kidney to the bladder.
  • Bladder: The storage tank. It's a muscular bag that stores urine until you're ready to go.
  • Urethra: The exit pipe. This is the tube that releases urine from the bladder out of the body.

Don't forget the... LUNGS!

That's right! Your lungs are excretory organs too. They excrete carbon dioxide every time you breathe out.

‚öôÔ∏è How Urine is Made (The Journey)

Follow the flow! Urine production is a clear 4-step process. (Hover over the cards to flip them!)

Step 1: The Filter

The KIDNEY filters the blood. It pulls out urea, plus extra water and salts.

Step 2: The Tubes

The waste fluid (now called urine) flows from the kidney down the URETERS.

Step 3: Storage

The BLADDER fills up and stores the urine.

Step 4: The Exit

Urine is released from the bladder and flows out of the body through the URETHRA.

What's Excreted?

The body excretes several key waste substances:

Waste Substance Excreted By...
Urea Kidneys (as part of urine)
Carbon Dioxide Lungs (when you breathe out)
Water & Salts Kidneys (as part of urine, to balance the body)
