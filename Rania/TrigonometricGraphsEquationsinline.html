Trigonometric Graphs

The Sine Graph: y = sin x

Please refer to the diagram of the sine graph.

  • It's a wave that oscillates between 1 and -1.
  • The period is 360¬∞ (it repeats every 360¬∞).
  • It passes through the origin (0, 0).
  • It hits key points at 90¬∞ intervals: (90, 1), (180, 0), (270, -1), (360, 0).

The Cosine Graph: y = cos x

Please refer to the diagram of the cosine graph.

  • Also a wave oscillating between 1 and -1.
  • The period is also 360¬∞.
  • It passes through the y-axis at (0, 1).
  • Key points: (90, 0), (180, -1), (270, 0), (360, 1).
  • It's a translation of the sine graph!

The Tangent Graph: y = tan x

Please refer to the diagram of the tangent graph.

  • It has a shorter period of 180¬∞.
  • It does not have a maximum or minimum value.
  • It has asymptotes at x = 90¬∞, 270¬∞, etc. An asymptote is a line the graph gets closer and closer to but never touches.
  • It passes through the origin (0, 0).
