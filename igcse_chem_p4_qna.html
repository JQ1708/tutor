(a) The interconversion between yellow aqueous potassium chromate(VI) and orange aqueous

potassium dichromate(VI) is a reversible reaction dependent on pH.

(yellow) (orange)

solution Y (an equilibrium mixture).

Answer

  • Adding sulfuric acid: The equilibrium moves to the right because the concentration of

reacts with/neutralises the sulfuric acid. The solution turns more yellow.

  • 70% of 300 cm³ = 210 cm³

There are fewer moles/molecules of gas on the left-hand side, so increasing pressure shifts

the equilibrium to the left.

(b) (iii) If the temperature is decreased, the yield of hydrogen decreases. What does

this indicate?

Answer

The forward reaction is endothermic.

(b) (iv) Why is a catalyst used in this reaction?

Answer

It increases the rate of reaction.

  • The equilibrium shifts in the direction of fewer molecules/moles, which is to the left.
