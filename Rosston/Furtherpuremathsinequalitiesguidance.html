Guidance for Graphical Questions (Q1 & Q4)

Both Question 1 and Question 4 require a similar graphical approach.

Step 1: Drawing the Lines

For each equation, find two points on the line. The easiest way is to find where the line crosses the x-axis (set y=0) and the y-axis (set x=0).

For Q1, you need to draw y = -x - 1, y = 3x - 9, and 2y = x + 7.

For Q4, you need to draw y = 3x - 3, 3x + 2y = 12, and y = -1.

Step 2: Identifying the Region R

To find the region R, pick a test point (like (0, 0)) and see if it satisfies each inequality. For example, in Q1, for y ≥ -x - 1, substitute (0, 0): 0 ≥ -0 - 1, which is 0 ≥ -1. This is true, so you shade the side of the line y = -x - 1 that contains (0, 0).

Do this for all three inequalities. The region where all shading overlaps is R.

Step 3: Finding Vertices

The region R will be a polygon (a triangle in these cases). The corners of this shape are called vertices. Find the coordinates of each vertex by solving the equations of the two lines that intersect to create it.

For Q1, you should have three vertices. One is at (2, -3). Find the other two!

For Q4, you should also have three vertices. One is at (?, -1).

Step 4: Finding Greatest / Least Value

The greatest or least value of P will always occur at one of the vertices. Substitute the (x, y) coordinates of each vertex you found into the given expression for P.
