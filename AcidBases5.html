(iv) The equation for the reaction is shown. Ca(OH)₂ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + 2H₂O. 20.0 cm³ of 0.0500 mol/dm³ HCl reacts with the 25.0 cm³ of Ca(OH)₂. Determine the concentration of Ca(OH)₂ in g/dm³.

Tap to reveal answer

Moles of HCl = 0.0500 x (20.0/1000) = 0.00100 mol.

Mole ratio Ca(OH)₂:HCl is 1:2. Moles of Ca(OH)₂ = 0.00100 / 2 = 0.000500 mol.

Concentration of Ca(OH)₂ (mol/dm³) = 0.000500 / (25.0/1000) = 0.0200 mol/dm³.

Mᵣ of Ca(OH)₂ = 40 + 2(16+1) = 74.

Concentration of Ca(OH)₂ (g/dm³) = 0.0200 x 74 = 1.48 g/dm³.

Anode: Copper

(c) Calculate the maximum mass of copper(II) sulfate crystals, CuSO₄·5H₂O, that can form from 50.0 cm³ of 0.200mol/dm³ dilute sulfuric acid.

Tap to reveal answer

Moles of H₂SO₄ = 0.200 x (50/1000) = 0.01 mol.

From the equation CuO + H₂SO₄ + 4H₂O → CuSO₄·5H₂O, the mole ratio is 1:1. Moles of CuSO₄·5H₂O = 0.01 mol.

Mᵣ of CuSO₄·5H₂O = 250. Mass = 0.01 x 250 = 2.5 g.

(d) State one observation that indicates the copper(II) oxide is in excess in step 1.

Tap to reveal answer

(e) Give a reason why the rate of reaction decreases without heating. Explain your answer in terms of particles.

Tap to reveal answer

Without heating, the particles have less kinetic energy. This leads to fewer collisions per unit time, and a smaller proportion of collisions have energy greater than the activation energy, so fewer collisions are successful.
