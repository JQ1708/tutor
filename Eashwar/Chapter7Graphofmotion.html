Here is a complete step-by-step guide for every question from Gerak Kerja 57 to 63. Take your time and try to fill in the missing values yourself!

Gerak Kerja 57: Drawing Distance-Time Graphs

General Guidance: For all graphing questions, first draw the axes. The horizontal axis (x-axis) is for Time, and the vertical axis (y-axis) is for Distance. Choose a scale for each axis that fits the data from the table. Plot each (Time, Distance) pair as a point, then connect the points with straight lines.

1. Mei Lin's journey to the shop.

Draw a graph with Time (0 to 5 mins) on the x-axis and Distance (0 to 60 m) on the y-axis. Plot the points (0,0), (1,12), (2,24), etc., and connect them.

2. Mei Lin's return journey.

Draw a graph with Time (0 to 10 mins) on the x-axis and Distance (0 to 60 m) on the y-axis. Plot the points (0,60), (2,36), (4,24), (6,24), etc. Note that the segment between t=4 and t=6 will be horizontal, showing she stopped.

3. Motion of a particle, s = t + 8.

Step 1: First, create a table of values for a few points in the 10-second period.
When t=0, s = 0 + 8 = 8. Point is (0, 8).
When t=10, s = 10 + 8 = 18. Point is (10, 18).
Step 2: Draw your axes, plot these two points, and connect them with a straight line.

Gerak Kerja 58: Interpreting Distance-Time Graphs

1. Car journey from Town A to B and back.

(a) Speed = Gradient of the line from O to the peak. Speed = (Change in Distance) ÷ (Change in Time) = (120 - 0) ÷ (2 - 0) = 60 km/h.
(b) Total distance = Distance to B + Distance back = 120 km + 120 km = 240 km.
(c) The car travels 120 km in (3.5 - 2) = 1.5 hours. Its speed is 120 ÷ 1.5 = 80 km/h.
(d) Average speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time = 240 km ÷ 3.5 h = 68.57 km/h.

2. Lorry journey from warehouse to office.

(a) Distance to Town P is the distance covered in the first 2 hours. This is the initial distance minus the distance at t=2. Distance = 180 - 60 = 120 km.
(b) The lorry stopped when the graph is horizontal. This is from t=2 to t=5. Duration = 5 - 2 = 3 hours.
(c) The lorry travels 120 km in 2 hours at a constant speed of 120 ÷ 2 = 60 km/h.
(d) Speed from P to office = Gradient of the last segment. Speed = (60 - 0) ÷ (7 - 5) = 60 ÷ 2 = 30 km/h.

Gerak Kerja 59: Problem Solving with Distance-Time Graphs

1. Journey of a bus and a car.

(a) Bus average speed = Total distance ÷ Total time = 320 km ÷ 4 h = 80 km/h.
(b) Step 1: Find the speed of the car (gradient of line MN). Speed = (320 - 0) ÷ (4 - 0) = 80 km/h.
Step 2: The car starts at (0, 320) and travels towards X. The question asks for time `t` when the car is at distance 200 from X.
Time = Distance ÷ Speed = (320 - 200) ÷ 80 = 120 ÷ 80 = 1.5 hours. So, t = 1.5.

2. Ahmad and Fuad's journey.

(a) Ahmad's total distance = 20 km (to station) + 20 km (back) = 40 km.
Ahmad's total time = 60 mins = 1 hour.
Average speed = Total distance ÷ Total time = 40 ÷ 1 = 40 km/h.
(b) Fuad's journey is QS. Speed is 60 km/h.
Time taken = 80 - 40 = 40 mins = 40/60 hours = 2/3 hours.
Distance = Speed × Time = 60 × (2/3) = 40 km.
This distance is from point Q (20km from start) to point S (d km from start).
So, d - 20 = 40. Therefore, d = 60 km.

Gerak Kerja 61: Interpreting Speed-Time Graphs

Part A: Calculate Distance Travelled

Key Concept: Distance travelled is the Area Under the Speed-Time Graph. You can split the area into rectangles, triangles, and trapeziums.

1. Area = Area of trapezium = ½ × (sum of parallel sides) × height = ½ × (2 + 8) × (10 - 4) ... Wait, that's not right. Split it.
Area = Area of trapezium (0-4s) + Area of trapezium (4-10s) = [½ × (2+8) × 4] + [½ × (8+11) × (10-4)] = 20 + 57 = 77 m.

2. Area = Area of trapezium (0-3s) + Area of rectangle (3-7s) + Area of triangle (7-11s)
= [½ × (20+16) × 3] + [16 × (7-3)] + [½ × (11-7) × 16] = 54 + 64 + 32 = 150 m.

3. Area = Area of trapezium = ½ × ( (8-3) + 10 ) × 16 = ½ × (5+10) × 16 = 120 m.

4. Area = Area of trapezium (0-2s) + Area of rectangle (2-6s) + Area of trapezium (6-9s)
= [½ × (8+12) × 2] + [12 × (6-2)] + [½ × (12+18) × (9-6)] = 20 + 48 + 45 = 113 m.

5. Break into 4 shapes. Area = [½×(3+12)×4] + [½×(12+7)×2] + [7×3] + [½×3×7] = 30 + 19 + 21 + 10.5 = 80.5 m.

Part B: Describe the Motion

Key Concept: The gradient (slope) of a speed-time graph is the acceleration (rate of change of speed).

(a) First 5s: The particle accelerates. Acceleration = (24 - 6) ÷ (5 - 0) = 3.6 m/s².

(b) Next 7s (from t=5 to t=12): The speed is constant. It moves at a uniform speed of 24 m/s.

(c) Last 5s (from t=12 to t=17): The particle decelerates. Deceleration = (0 - 24) ÷ (17 - 12) = -4.8 m/s².

Gerak Kerja 62: Problem Solving with Speed-Time Graphs

1. Motion of a particle for 11 seconds.

(a) Distance in first 8s = Area up to t=8 = [½ × (4+8) × 2] + [8 × (8-2)] = 12 + 48 = 60 m.
(b) Uniform speed is the speed on the flat section = 8 m/s.
(c) Rate of change = gradient.
Last 3s: (v - 8) ÷ (11 - 8) = (v-8)/3.
First 2s: (8 - 4) ÷ (2 - 0) = 2.
Set up equation: (v-8)/3 = 2 × (2) => v-8 = 12 => v = 20.

2. Motion of a particle for 10 seconds.

(a) Rate of change in first 4s = 4. So, (22 - u) ÷ (4 - 0) = 4 => 22 - u = 16 => u = 6.
(b) Rate of change in last 6s = (0 - 22) ÷ (10 - 4) = -22 ÷ 6 = -3.67 m/s².
(c) Total Distance = Area of trapezium = ½ × (u + 22) × 4 + Area of triangle = [½ × (6+22) × 4] + [½ × (10-4) × 22] = 56 + 66 = 122 m.

Gerak Kerja 63: SPM Clone Questions

1. Ali's trip.

(a) Stop duration is the horizontal part of the graph, from 0830 to 0900. This is 30 minutes or 0.5 hours.
(b) Total Distance = 30km + 30km = 60km. Total Time = 1000 - 0800 = 2 hours.
Average speed = 60 km ÷ 2 h = 30 km/h.

2. Particle motion for m seconds.

(a) Uniform speed is on the flat section = 8 m/s.
(b) Rate of change in first 6s = (8 - 0) ÷ (6 - 0) = 1.33 m/s².
(c) Dist(0-6s) = Area = ½ × 6 × 8 = 24 m.
Dist(9-m s) = Area = ½ × (8+16) × (m-9).
Set up equation: 24 = ½ × [½ × (24) × (m-9)] => 24 = 6(m-9) => 4 = m-9 => m = 13.

3. Marina's drive.

(a)(i) At t=66 mins (8:06), she drove 90km. Graph is distance FROM Melaka. So m = 160 - 90 = 70. She rests until 8:30. Time from 7:00 to 8:30 is 90 mins. So n = 90.
(a)(ii) The graph is complete up to point (90, 70). The journey ends when distance is 0. The graph shows the timeline ends at 130 mins. So, draw a straight line from the point (90, 70) to the point (130, 0).
(b) Total distance = 160 km. Total time = 130 minutes = 130/60 hours.
Average speed = 160 ÷ (130/60) = (160 × 60) ÷ 130 = 73.85 km/h.
