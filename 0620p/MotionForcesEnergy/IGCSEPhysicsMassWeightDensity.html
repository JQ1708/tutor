Mass & Weight

What is Mass?
  • Mass is defined as a measure of the quantity of matter in an object at rest relative to the observer.
  • Consequently, mass is the property of an object that resists change in motion. The greater the mass, the more difficult it is to speed it up, slow it down, or change its direction.
  • Mass is a scalar quantity (it has magnitude but no direction).
  • The unit of mass is the kilogram (kg).
  • Sometimes mass is given in grams (g). Remember the conversions:
    1000 g = 1 kg
    1 g = 0.001 kg
  • To convert from g to kg, divide by 1000.
  • To convert from kg to g, multiply by 1000.
What is Weight?
  • Weight is the gravitational force on an object with mass.
  • Since weight is a force, it is a vector quantity (it has both magnitude and direction).
  • Weight is measured in newtons (N).
  • Weight is measured using a calibrated spring balance or newton meter.
  • Mass is measured using a pan balance or top-pan balance.
How are Mass and Weight related?

An object's weight is directly proportional to its mass. This relationship is given by the equation:

Weight = mass × gravitational field strength
W = m × g
  • W = Weight in newtons (N)
  • m = mass in kilograms (kg)
  • g = gravitational field strength in N/kg

Worked Example

An object has a mass of 90 kg. Calculate the weight of the object on Earth.

Step 1: List the known quantities.

  • Mass, m = 90 kg
  • Gravitational field strength, g = 9.8 N/kg

Step 2: Write down the relevant equation.

W = m × g

Step 3: Substitute in the values.

W = 90 × 9.8 = 882 N

What is Gravitational Field Strength (g)?
  • It is defined as the force per unit mass exerted by a gravitational field on an object.
  • On Earth, the value is approximately g ‚âà 9.8 N/kg.
  • The value of 'g' varies depending on your location. For example, on the Moon, 'g' is about 1.6 N/kg.
  • This means an object's weight will be about 6 times smaller on the Moon than on Earth.
  • Crucially, because weight depends on 'g', an object's weight changes with location, but its mass always stays the same.

Density

What is Density?
  • Density is defined as the mass per unit volume of a substance.
  • Objects made from low-density materials (like foam or air) have a low mass for their size.
  • Objects made from high-density materials (like metal or rock) have a high mass for their size.

What does density depend on?
  • The density of an object depends on the material it is made of.
  • Density does NOT depend on the size or shape of the object. A small gold ring and a large gold bar have the same density because they are both made of gold.
  • Density is an intrinsic property of a substance.

Measuring Density

Measuring Density of a Regular Object
  1. Measure the mass of the object using a top-pan balance.
  2. Measure its dimensions (length, width, height, etc.) using a ruler or calipers.
  3. Calculate the volume using the correct formula (e.g., V = l × w × h for a cuboid).
  4. Use the density formula ρ = m/V to calculate its density.

Floating

Why do things float?
  • An object will float in a fluid (a liquid or a gas) if its average density is less than the density of the fluid.
  • An object will sink in a fluid if its average density is greater than the density of the fluid.
  • For example, wood floats in water because its density (~0.7 g/cm¬≥) is less than water's density (1.0 g/cm¬≥). A rock sinks because its density (~2.5 g/cm¬≥) is greater than water's.
Floating Liquids (Extended Tier Only)
  • If you pour together different liquids that do not mix, they will form layers based on their density.
  • The liquid with the lowest density will float on top.
  • The liquid with the highest density will sink to the bottom.
  • Please refer to the diagram showing liquids of different densities (like honey, soap, water, oil) stacked in a container.

Worked Example

Liquid A has a density of 0.76 g/cm¬≥ and liquid B has a density of 0.93 g/cm¬≥. If the two liquids do not mix, which liquid will float on top of the other?

Answer: The liquid with the lowest density will float. Since 0.76 is less than 0.93, Liquid A will float on top of Liquid B.
