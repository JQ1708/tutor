Pressure & Forces

What is Pressure?

Pressure is defined as the
force per unit area.

Units of Pressure

Pressure is measured in Pascals (Pa).

1 Pascal is equivalent to 1 Newton per metre squared.

1 Pa = 1 N/m2

The Pressure Equation

p =
F A

p = pressure in Pascals (Pa) or N/m2

F = force in Newtons (N)

A = area in metres squared (m2)

Applications of Pressure

Tractors üöú

Tractors have large, wide tyres to spread the weight (force) of the tractor over a large area. This reduces the pressure on the ground, preventing the tractor from sinking into soft mud.

Football Boots ‚öΩ

Football boots have studs, which have a very small surface area. The player's weight (force) is concentrated on these studs, creating a very large pressure. This allows the studs to sink into the ground, providing grip.

Sharp Knife üî™

A sharp knife has a very thin blade. This concentrates the force applied to the handle onto a tiny area. The resulting high pressure is what allows the knife to cut through materials.

Pressure in a Liquid

What causes pressure in a liquid?

The pressure at a certain point within a liquid is caused by the weight of the column of liquid directly above that point.

As the depth of the liquid increases, the amount (and therefore the weight) of liquid above it increases. The greater the weight, the greater the pressure.

[Please refer to the diagram showing the column of liquid creating pressure.]

Key Properties

  • Pressure increases with depth.
  • Pressure increases with density.
  • Pressure is the same at the same depth within a liquid.
  • Pressure acts in all directions at a point.
  • Force from pressure is exerted evenly across a whole surface.

Factors Affecting Pressure

The pressure due to a column of liquid depends on:

  • The depth (or height) of the column, h
  • The density of the liquid, œÅ (rho)
  • The gravitational field strength, g

Liquid Pressure Equation

Δp = ρ × g × Δh

Δp = change in pressure in Pascals (Pa)

ρ = density of the liquid in kg/m3

g = gravitational field strength in N/kg

Δh = change in height (depth) of the column in metres (m)

Examiner's Tip: Total Pressure

Some exam questions will ask for the total pressure at a certain depth. In this case, you must remember to add the atmospheric pressure to the pressure calculated from the liquid.

Total Pressure Equation

Ptotal = Patmospheric + (ρ × g × h)

Atmospheric pressure at sea level is approximately 1.01 × 105 Pa.

You must also be able to recall the main liquid pressure equation and be comfortable rearranging it for any variable!
