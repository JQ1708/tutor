Photosynthesis

  • Plants are producers; they make their own food.
  • Uses energy from light absorbed by a green substance called chlorophyll (found in chloroplasts).
  • Process converts carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen.
carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen
(Requires light and chlorophyll)
  • It is an endothermic chemical reaction.

Leaf Adaptations

  • Palisade cells: contain many chloroplasts for photosynthesis.
  • Stoma (plural: stomata): tiny holes allowing gases to move by diffusion.
  • Guard cells: control the opening and closing of a stoma.
  • Xylem cells: form hollow tubes carrying water into the leaf.

Plant Carbohydrates & Testing

  • Glucose is a sugar (small carbohydrate) used for aerobic respiration.
  • Plants store energy as starch (a large, insoluble carbohydrate).
  • Testing for Starch:
    1. Boil leaf in water to soften.
    2. Heat in ethanol to remove green chlorophyll.
    3. Add iodine solution. Changes from orange to blue-black if starch is present.

Transport of Water

  • Root hair cells: have huge surface area to absorb water and minerals quickly.
  • Xylem vessels: dead cells forming hollow tubes, stiffened with lignin, carrying water up the stem.
  • Transpiration: the loss of water vapour through the stomata on the leaf surface.
  • Without water, the sap vacuole shrinks, cells lose shape, and the plant wilts.

Mineral Salts

Minerals are needed in small amounts for healthy growth. Deficiency causes issues.

Element Why it's needed Deficiency Symptom
Nitrogen (Nitrates) To make proteins for plant growth and repair. Reduced plant growth, yellow leaves, weak stems.
Magnesium To make chlorophyll (green pigment). Leaves turn yellow.

Farmers use fertilisers (natural like manure or artificial) to add minerals to soil. Excess can wash into rivers and harm ecosystems.

Photosynthesis
A series of chemical reactions in which plants convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen using light energy.
Chlorophyll
The green substance that absorbs light energy for photosynthesis, found inside chloroplasts.
Stoma (Stomata)
A small hole in a leaf formed between two guard cells, allowing gases to move in and out.
Starch
A large, insoluble carbohydrate made by plants to store energy. Tested using iodine solution (turns blue-black).
Transpiration
The loss of water vapour through the stomata on the surface of the leaves.
Xylem
Dead cells forming hollow vessels/tubes. Carries water and minerals from roots to leaves.
Wilting
When a plant becomes floppy because its cells lose shape due to a lack of water pushing on the sap vacuole.
