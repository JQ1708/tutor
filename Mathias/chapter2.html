Excretion

  • Excretion is getting rid of waste substances made inside an organism's cells.
  • Lungs excrete carbon dioxide from respiration.
  • Kidneys excrete urea, a waste made when breaking down proteins.

The Excretory (Renal) System

  • Also called the renal system. Its job is to filter blood and remove wastes.
  • Kidneys: Clean the blood by removing urea and some water to make urine.
  • Ureters: Tubes that carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder.
  • Bladder: Stores urine like a balloon.
  • Urethra: A tube that carries urine out of the body when you urinate.

Kidney Disease & Dialysis

  • Damaged kidney filters can allow proteins to leak into the urine.
  • Dialysis is a medical treatment that filters the blood of people whose kidneys are failing.
  • A dialysis machine pumps blood through fine filters to remove urea before returning it to the body.
