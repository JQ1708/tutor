Atomic Structure

  • Atoms are very small. They have a central nucleus surrounded by electron shells.
  • Nucleus contains Protons (positive charge) and Neutrons (no charge).
  • Shells contain Electrons (negative charge).
  • Atomic Number = Number of protons (also = number of electrons in a neutral atom).
  • Mass Number = Total number of protons + neutrons.
  • Electron Arrangement: 1st shell holds 2, 2nd holds 8, 3rd holds 8.

The Periodic Table

  • Developed by Dmitri Mendeleev in 1869 based on properties; he left gaps for undiscovered elements.
  • Elements are arranged in order of increasing atomic number.
  • Periods (rows): The period number tells you how many electron shells the atom has.
  • Groups (columns): The group number tells you how many electrons are in the outer shell. Elements in the same group have similar chemical properties.

Trends in Groups (e.g., Group 1)

  • Group 1 elements are alkali metals (Lithium, Sodium, Potassium, Rubidium, Caesium, Francium).
  • Chemical Reaction: They all react with water to produce hydrogen and an alkaline solution (metal hydroxide).
  • Example: sodium + water → sodium hydroxide + hydrogen
  • Reactivity Trend: They become more reactive as you go down the group (Lithium is slow, Potassium ignites, Caesium is explosive).
  • Physical Trend: Melting points decrease as you go down the group. They are also soft enough to cut with a knife.

Proton
A positively charged particle found in the nucleus of an atom.
Neutron
A particle with no charge (neutral) found in the nucleus of an atom.
Electron
A negatively charged particle that orbits the nucleus in electron shells.
Atomic Number
The number of protons in an atom (also equals the number of electrons). It determines the element.
Mass Number
The total number of protons + neutrons in the nucleus of an atom.
Group Number
The column in the Periodic Table. It tells you the number of electrons in the outer shell.
Period Number
The row in the Periodic Table. It tells you how many electron shells the atom has.
Alkali Metals
Group 1 elements. They are soft, reactive metals that react with water to produce hydrogen gas and an alkaline solution.
