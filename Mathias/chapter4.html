Resources & Adaptation

  • Organisms need resources (water, food, space) to survive in their environment.
  • An adaptation is a characteristic that helps an organism survive.
  • Desert (Fox, Cactus): Large ears (lose heat), sandy color, spines instead of leaves, thick waxy cover, quick spreading roots.
  • Polar (Snowy Owl): Thick feather layers, white camouflage cover, sharp talons.

Populations & Predators

  • A population is the number of individuals of one species in an area.
  • Affected by non-living (temperature, water, pollution) and living (diseases, competition, predators) factors.
  • Predator-Prey interactions: When prey numbers increase, predators have more food, so predator numbers increase. More predators eat more prey, so prey numbers drop, causing predator numbers to drop.

Extinction

  • When a species dies out completely, it becomes extinct. Causes include:
  • New diseases (e.g., chytrid fungus in frogs).
  • Invasive species: Compete for resources or eat native species (e.g., Brown tree snakes).
  • Hunting & Poaching for valuable parts (e.g., Rhinoceros horns).
  • Habitat destruction & Catastrophic events (meteors, global warming).
  • If a substance in the environment causes harm, it is pollution (e.g., plastics in oceans choking turtles).
