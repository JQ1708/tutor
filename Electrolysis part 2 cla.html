⚖️ Fuel Cells vs Petrol Engines

✅ ADVANTAGES

  • 🔄 Renewable (hydrogen from water)
  • 🌿 No pollution (only water produced)
  • ⚡ More energy per kilogram
  • 🔧 No moving parts = no power loss
  • 🤫 Quieter than petrol engines

❌ DISADVANTAGES

  • 🏭 Hydrogen production may use fossil fuels
  • ⚡ Electrolysis needs lots of electricity
  • 💰 Expensive materials
  • 🔥 Hydrogen is flammable/explosive
  • 🥶 Less efficient in cold temperatures
  • ⛽ Few hydrogen filling stations

🎯 Key Takeaways Summary:

  • Electroplating: Coating one metal with another using electrolysis
  • Anode: Pure metal that gets oxidized (loses electrons)
  • Cathode: Object being plated, where reduction occurs (gains electrons)
  • Uses: Corrosion resistance (galvanising) and improved appearance
  • Fuel Cells: Convert chemical energy to electrical energy
  • Hydrogen + Oxygen → Water + Electricity
  • Advantages: Clean, renewable, efficient, quiet
  • Disadvantages: Expensive, storage issues, infrastructure challenges
