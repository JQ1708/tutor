What is Adaptation?

+

An adaptation is a feature of a species that helps it to survive and reproduce in a particular ecosystem.

What Factors Affect Populations?

+

Many factors in an ecosystem can affect the size of a population. These include:

Temperature Competition Predation Water Availability

Predator-Prey Cycles

+

One of the biggest factors is predation. This creates a cycle between the hunter (predator) and the hunted (prey).

  • In the example of wolves and hares:
    • The wolf is the predator.
    • The hare is the prey.
  • When the population of hares (prey) increases, the population of wolves (predator) increases shortly after.
  • Why? This happens because there is more food (hares) available for the wolves to eat, so more wolves can survive and reproduce.
  • What if... the hares all got a disease and became extinct?
    • The wolf population would decrease and could also become extinct.
  • Why? The wolves would have no food source.

Extinction

+

Sometimes, factors can cause a whole species to die out. This is called extinction.

Scientists investigate factors that could have caused species like the Camelops (an extinct camel) to become extinct.

Possible causes of extinction include:

  • Hunting
  • Pollution
  • A permanent change in climate (scientists investigate how this could cause extinction, for example, by changing the availability of food or water).
