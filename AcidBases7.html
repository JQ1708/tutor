Question 47

(0620/43_Winter_2023_Q4) - Acids And Bases, Using Moles

This question is about sulfuric acid and salts that are made from sulfuric acid.

(a) Zinc reacts with dilute sulfuric acid. Aqueous zinc sulfate is one of the products.

(i) Name the limiting reactant.

Tap to reveal answer

Dilute sulfuric acid

(ii) State two observations that indicate the zinc is in excess.

Tap to reveal answer

1. A solid remains / solid is undissolved.

2. The bubbling/effervescence/fizzing stops.

(iii) Name the filtrate.

Tap to reveal answer

Aqueous zinc sulfate

(iv) Name two compounds which both react with dilute sulfuric acid to produce aqueous zinc sulfate.

Tap to reveal answer

Any two from: zinc oxide, zinc carbonate, zinc hydroxide.

(b) Zinc sulfate crystals are produced by heating aqueous zinc sulfate until a saturated solution is formed.

(i) State what is meant by the term saturated solution.

Tap to reveal answer

A solution containing the maximum amount of solute dissolved at a given temperature.

(ii) Explain why crystals form when the saturated solution cools down.

Tap to reveal answer

The solubility of the salt decreases as the temperature decreases, so the excess solute comes out of solution as crystals.

(c) Nickel(II) sulfate crystals contain water of crystallisation.

(i) State the term used to describe crystals that contain water of crystallisation.

Tap to reveal answer

Hydrated

(ii) State why step 4 is repeated until there is no change in mass.

Tap to reveal answer

To ensure all the water of crystallisation has been removed.

(iii) In an experiment, 0.454 g of nickel(II) sulfate crystals, NiSO₄·xH₂O is used. The mass of anhydrous nickel(II) sulfate, NiSO₄, remaining is 0.310g. Determine the value of x.

Tap to reveal answer

Moles of NiSO₄ = 0.310 / 155 = 0.002 mol.

Mass of H₂O = 0.454 - 0.310 = 0.144 g.

Moles of H₂O = 0.144 / 18 = 0.008 mol.

Ratio of moles NiSO₄ : H₂O = 0.002 : 0.008 = 1 : 4. So, x = 4.
