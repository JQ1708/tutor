Balanced & Unbalanced Forces

Balanced & Unbalanced Forces

  • Force: A push, pull, or twist. Measured in Newtons (N).
  • Balanced Forces: Equal in size, opposite in direction. Cause no change in movement.
  • Unbalanced Forces: Unequal in size or not opposite. Always cause a change in movement.

Effects of Unbalanced Forces

Effects of Unbalanced Forces

An unbalanced force changes an object's motion by:

  • 1. Speeding Up: Force in same direction as motion.
  • 2. Slowing Down: Force in opposite direction to motion.
  • 3. Changing Direction: Force at an angle to motion.

Force Diagrams

Force Diagrams

  • Arrows: Represent forces.
  • Arrow Direction: Shows the force's direction.
  • Arrow Length: Represents the force's size. A longer arrow means a bigger force.

Speed & Its Calculation

Speed & Its Calculation

  • Speed: How fast an object moves. Unit: m/s.
  • Distance: How far it moves. Unit: m.
  • Time: How long it takes. Unit: s.
Speed = Distance / Time

Distance-Time Graphs

Distance-Time Graphs

  • Horizontal Line: Stationary.
  • Upward Slope: Constant speed away from start.
  • Downward Slope: Constant speed back to start.
  • Steepness: Steeper line = faster speed.

Pressure

Pressure

  • Pressure: Force pushing on a certain area.
  • High Pressure: Large force or a small area (sharp knife).
  • Low Pressure: Small force or a large area (snowshoes).
Pressure = Force / Area

Pressure in Liquids & Gases

Pressure in Liquids & Gases

  • Cause: Moving particles colliding with surfaces.
  • In Liquids: Pressure increases with depth.
  • In Gases: Pressure increases with more particles or higher temperature.

Diffusion

Diffusion

  • Definition: Particles moving from a high concentration area to a low concentration area.
  • Happens In: Liquids and gases.
  • Faster When: Temperature is higher.
