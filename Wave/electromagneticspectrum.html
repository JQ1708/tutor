1. The EM Spectrum: An Overview

A continuous spectrum of waves arranged by frequency and wavelength. They all share some amazing properties.

Core Properties

What do all EM waves have in common?

  • They are all transverse waves.
  • They can all travel through a vacuum.
  • They all travel at the same speed in a vacuum (the speed of light).

3. Dangers of Electromagnetic Waves

Higher frequency waves carry more energy and can be more dangerous. The potential for harm increases up the spectrum.

Microwaves

Infrared

Ultraviolet

High doses can damage surface cells, leading to sunburn and an increased risk of skin cancer. Can also damage the eyes, leading to conditions like cataracts.

X-rays & Gamma Rays

These are ionising radiation because they have enough energy to knock electrons out of atoms. This can damage or kill living cells, cause mutations in genes, and lead to cancer. This is why exposure is minimised in medical settings (e.g., lead aprons).

4. Radio Waves & Communication

How do we generate and receive information using radio waves?

Producing Radio Waves

Creating the signal.

Radio waves are produced by oscillations in electrical circuits. When electrons are made to oscillate in a transmitter aerial, they create changing electric and magnetic fields, which radiate outwards as a radio wave.

Receiving Radio Waves

Detecting the signal.

When these waves are absorbed by a receiver aerial, they make electrons in the receiver oscillate. This creates a changing electric current which can be processed as information (e.g., sound or pictures).

5. Analogue & Digital Signals

EM waves are perfect for sending information over long distances. How that information is encoded makes a big difference.

Analogue Signals

The continuous wave.

The amplitude and/or frequency of the wave vary continuously. They can take any value within a range. They are prone to picking up noise (unwanted interference), which degrades the signal quality and is difficult to remove.

Digital Signals

The binary code.

Consist of a series of pulses that are either ON (1) or OFF (0). They have only two possible values. Noise is less of a problem because the signal can be regenerated perfectly by a regenerator, restoring it to its original quality.

Advantages of Digital Signals

  • Minimal Noise: The signal can be accurately regenerated, removing interference.
  • Increased Range: Perfect regeneration allows signals to travel further without losing quality.
  • Higher Data Rate: More information (bits) can be transmitted per second.
  • Error Checking: Extra data can be added so that the signal can be checked for and correct errors.
