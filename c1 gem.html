What is a Solution?

What is a Solution?

  • Solute: The substance that dissolves (e.g., sugar).
  • Solvent: The liquid it dissolves in (e.g., water).
  • Solution: The mixture formed when a solute dissolves in a solvent.
  • Solutions are **transparent** (you can see through them), but not always colourless.

Conservation of Mass

Conservation of Mass

  • When a solute dissolves, its particles spread out but they don't disappear.
  • No mass is lost during dissolving. The total mass is **conserved**.
Mass of Solute + Mass of Solvent = Mass of Solution

Solubility

Solubility

  • Soluble: A substance that CAN dissolve in a solvent (e.g., salt in water).
  • Insoluble: A substance that CANNOT dissolve in a solvent (e.g., sand in water).
  • Saturated Solution: A solution that cannot dissolve any more solute at that temperature.

Temperature & Solubility

Temperature & Solubility

  • For most solid solutes, solubility **increases** as the temperature of the solvent increases.
  • This means you can dissolve more sugar in hot water than in cold water.

Interpreting Chromatograms

Interpreting Chromatograms

  • Pure Substance: Shows only **one spot**.
  • Mixture: Shows **multiple spots** at different heights.
  • The **higher** a spot travels, the **more soluble** the substance is in the solvent.
