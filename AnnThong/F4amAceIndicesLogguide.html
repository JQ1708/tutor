5.1 Indices and Laws of Indices

1. Simplify 54n+2 × 25n56n-1

Step 1: Express all terms in the same base (base 5). Note that 25 = 52.
54n+2 × (52)n56n-1 = 54n+2 × 52n56n-1
Step 2: Simplify the numerator using the law am × an = am+n.
= 5(4n+2) + 2n56n-1 = 56n+256n-1
Step 3: Simplify the fraction using the law aman = am-n.
= 5(6n+2) - (6n-1) = 56n+2 - 6n + 1 = 53

2. Express 5n+2 - 5n+1 - 5n in the simplest form.

Step 1: Split the terms using the law am+n = am × an.
= (5n × 52) - (5n × 51) - 5n
Step 2: Factor out the common term, 5n.
= 5n × (52 - 51 - 1)
Step 3: Calculate the value inside the bracket.
= 5n × (25 - 5 - 1) = 5n × (19)

3. If 56n × 94n × 152n32n = k8n, find k.

Step 1: Express all bases as prime numbers (3 and 5).
56n × (32)4n × (3 × 5)2n32n = 56n × 38n × 32n × 52n32n
Step 2: Group terms with the same base and simplify.
= 56n+2n × 38n+2n-2n = 58n × 38n
Step 3: Combine the terms using the law (a × b)m = am × bm.
= (5 × 3)8n = 158n. By comparison, k = 15.

4. Simplify 25m × 10m+12m-1 × 52+3m

Step 1: Express all bases as prime numbers (2 and 5).
(52)m × (2 × 5)m+12m-1 × 52+3m = 52m × 2m+1 × 5m+12m-1 × 52+3m
Step 2: Group terms with base 2 and base 5.
= 2m+12m-1 × 52m × 5m+152+3m = 2(m+1) - (m-1) × 5(2m+m+1) - (2+3m)
Step 3: Simplify the powers.
= 22 × 5-1 = 45

5. Simplify 82n-4 × 4n+164-n × 214n-7

Step 1: Express all terms in base 2.
(23)2n-4 × (22)n+1(26)-n × 214n-7 = 26n-12 × 22n+22-6n × 214n-7
Step 2: Simplify the numerator and the denominator separately.
= 2(6n-12)+(2n+2)2(-6n)+(14n-7) = 28n - 1028n - 7
Step 3: Simplify the fraction.
= 2(8n-10) - (8n-7) = 2-3 = 18

6. Given 5n+2 - 5n - 25 × (5n-1) = k × (5n), find k.

Step 1: Express all terms with 5n.
(5n × 52) - 5n - (52) × (5n × 5-1) = k × (5n)
Step 2: Simplify the expression.
25 × (5n) - 5n - 52-1 × (5n) = 25 × (5n) - 5n - 5 × (5n) = k × (5n)
Step 3: Factor out 5n on the left side.
5n × (25 - 1 - 5) = 5n × (19) = k × (5n). Thus, k = 19.
