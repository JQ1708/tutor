Gas Exchange

Gas Exchange

  • Occurs in the **alveoli** (air sacs).
  • **Oxygen** diffuses from the air in the alveoli into the blood.
  • **Carbon Dioxide** diffuses from the blood into the air in the alveoli.
  • **Diffusion:** Movement of particles from an area of high concentration to low concentration.

Breathing Mechanics

Breathing Mechanics

  • Breathing In (Inhalation):
    • - Diaphragm **contracts** (moves down).
    • - Rib muscles **contract** (ribs move up & out).
    • - Chest volume **increases**, pressure decreases, air rushes in.
  • Breathing Out (Exhalation): The opposite happens - muscles **relax**, volume decreases, air is pushed out.

Respiration vs. Breathing

Respiration vs. Breathing

  • Breathing: The physical process of moving air in and out of the lungs.
  • Respiration: A **chemical reaction** inside every living **cell** to release energy from food.
  • Respiration happens in tiny organelles called **mitochondria**.

Components of Blood

Components of Blood

  • Plasma: The pale yellow liquid part. Transports glucose, CO₂, and cells.
  • Red Blood Cells: Transport oxygen. Contain **haemoglobin**. Have no nucleus to make more room.
  • White Blood Cells: Part of the immune system. Fight pathogens (germs).

How White Blood Cells Protect Us

How White Blood Cells Protect Us

  • White blood cells are our body's defenders against **pathogens** (disease-causing germs).
  • Phagocytosis: Some white blood cells change shape to surround and 'eat' pathogens.
  • Antibodies: Other white blood cells produce special proteins called antibodies that stick to pathogens and help destroy them.
